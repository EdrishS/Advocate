Introduction

Child crime law, also known as juvenile law or juvenile justice law, deals specifically with criminal offenses committed by minors. Here are some key points about child crime law:

Definition of Juvenile Offender: In most jurisdictions, a juvenile offender is a person under a specified age limit (often 18 years old) who commits an offense that would be considered a crime if committed by an adult.

Legal System: Juvenile justice systems are designed to handle cases involving minors separately from adults. The focus is often on rehabilitation and the welfare of the child, rather than punishment.

Legal Proceedings: Juvenile cases may proceed through specialized courts or through different procedures than adult criminal cases. The goal is to address the needs of the child while ensuring public safety.
