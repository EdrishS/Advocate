Introduction

Background: In 1973, the landmark case of Roe v. Wade reached the United States Supreme Court, fundamentally shaping health care law and women's reproductive rights in the country.

Legal Issue: The central issue in Roe v. Wade was the constitutionality of Texas laws that criminalized most abortions, except those performed to save the life of the mother. Norma McCorvey (under the alias "Jane Roe"), a pregnant woman from Texas, challenged these laws on the grounds that they violated her constitutional right to privacy.

Court Decision: The Supreme Court, in a 7-2 decision, ruled in favor of Roe. The Court held that a woman's right to terminate her pregnancy falls within the right to privacy protected by the Fourteenth Amendment, which includes personal autonomy in matters relating to family, marriage, and childbearing. The Court established a framework dividing pregnancy into three trimesters, with increasing state interest in regulating abortions as pregnancy progresses.
