Introduction

Background: In 1884, the case of R v. Dudley and Stephens was heard in the United Kingdom, involving a moral and legal dilemma arising from a shipwreck.

Legal Issue: The central legal issue in the case was whether Dudley and Stephens could be justified in killing Parker under the circumstances of extreme necessity, known in legal terms as the defense of necessity or "duress of circumstances."

Court Decision: The case was tried at the Exeter Assizes in England. Dudley and Stephens were initially convicted of murder and sentenced to death. The judge ruled that killing Parker could not be justified as a necessity because there was no imminent threat to the lives of Dudley and Stephens at the moment they killed him. They had been adrift for several days and were not in immediate danger of death.
